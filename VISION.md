Vision & Origin
Energy is the currency of the future. My fundamental thought was simple: water as a medium carries immense, often untapped potential. Much like solar energy, flowing or dammed water is concentrated energy in the form of gravity and inertia. My goal was to find a way to harvest this 
potential to the maximum – scaled down to the smallest state: a single drop or a defined quantity of water.
The "Bloom" Concept
Imagine a river gradient or a dam. The water is accelerated in cascades and guided into a funnel so that it strikes exactly in the center of a flat cylinder. This impact generates circular oscillations (impulse energy).
To convert this energy efficiently, EAB-GENESIS utilizes three physical stages:
Central Impact: The strike is converted directly into electrical impulses via a piezo anvil.
The Wave: The resulting kinetic energy spreads radially and hits a specially shaped ramp (Exponential Cone Topology).
TENG Conversion: At the ramp, additional charge is harvested through interfacial friction (Triboelectric effect). The upper edge is designed to break the wave, preventing reflections (echoes) and collecting the water in a trough to feed it to the next level using the remaining 
gravitational energy.
Modularity & Sustainability
These modules are small, modular, and largely wear-free. Hundreds of these "Blooms" could be connected in series on the discharge side of a dam or used decentrally in nature without destroying the ecosystem.
